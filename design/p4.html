Fresh, clean tones are fused with the human touch of hand-crafted
finishes and functional objects at Aesop Covent Garden. In marrying a
cultivated aesthetic to the seasonal changeability of nature, it becomes
a fitting ode to a local narrative borne of the necessary rigour of urban
planning and the traditional role that market commerce plays in a city.

Covent Garden is Aesop’s sixth store in London, representing an ongoing relationship not only with this
ancient city but also with French design studio Ciguë, who were responsible for the design. This fresh, cheery
space, resplendent in crisp hues of white and green, brings the architects’ artisanal savoir-faire to the fore,
while responding to multi-layered cultural cues of the surrounding neighbourhood – home to London’s first
public square and the historic open fresh produce market. Four distinct references formed the point of
departure: a Virginia Woolf quote; a Francis Bacon painting; a Henry Moore sculpture and an excerpt from
Beauty and the Beast. If the interiors are borne of disparate inspirations, the end product feels anything but
disconnected. Rather, consistent materials and colours create a purity of expression tempered by the
injection of nature’s unpredictability.
